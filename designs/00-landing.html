How Janus works

One workflow. Every step accountable.

01

Branded intake

Your logo, your link. Mobile-first form, two-consent FCRA screen, applicant pays the $30 Janus platform fee up front.

02

Identity first

Selfie + government ID via Stripe Identity. Stops synthetic-identity fraud at the door — before any paid report runs.

03

Income, evidenced

Three months of bank statements and two paystubs, parsed by OCR. Stacked across co-applicants, with month-over-month volatility flagged.

04

TransUnion — direct

Credit, criminal, and eviction are pulled by TransUnion SmartMove. The applicant pays TransUnion ~$40 directly. We never touch screening money.

05

References without phone tag

Tokenized links to current landlord and employer. Structured response forms. No login. Rate limited.

06

Decision with reasoning

Policy-driven recommendation with cited evidence. Override anytime — every override logged for fair-housing review and FCRA adverse-action notices.

Pricing

Two line items. No surprises.

The applicant pays the platform fee inside Janus. They pay the screening bureau separately on TransUnion. We don't bundle, mark up, or take a cut of the screening cost.

Property managers pay nothing per applicant during MVP — start free and add a team subscription when you outgrow it.

Policies, not gut calls

Configure thresholds once. Apply them across every property.

Set your income-to-rent ratio, credit floor, and deposit policy. Janus applies them consistently — and shows every applicant exactly which threshold drove the recommendation.

Section 8 vouchers adjust the income test to the tenant portion automatically (HUD-aligned). Eviction history routes to manual review — never auto-denied. Criminal flags require an individualized assessment per HUD 2016/2022 guidance.
